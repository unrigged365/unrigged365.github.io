## Executive Summary

The Dow/Gold ratio is one of the most powerful long-term valuation metrics in financial markets, measuring how many ounces of gold it takes to "buy" the Dow Jones Industrial Average. This ratio provides crucial insights into relative asset valuations: high ratios suggest stocks are expensive relative to gold and potentially overvalued, while low ratios indicate gold outperforming stocks, often signaling economic uncertainty.

The ratio has experienced three complete mega-cycles over the past 75 years:

1. **1950-1980**: Rise from 6.78 to peak at **1.57** (gold dominance)
2. **1980-1999**: Rise from 1.57 to peak at **41.22** (stock mania)
3. **1999-2011**: Fall from 41.22 to low at **7.77** (gold resurgence)
4. **2011-2025**: Current cycle - rose to 20.49 (2019), now falling to **~11.83** (2025)

The ratio is currently breaking below the critical 12.0 level, a historically significant threshold that has preceded major market turning points in 1929, 1973, and 2008. Each time this level was breached, gold subsequently outperformed stocks dramatically.

**Current Status (November 2025):** At approximately 11.83 (Dow ~47,337 / Gold ~$4,000), the ratio signals that capital is flowing from centralized assets (stocks) to decentralized assets (gold), suggesting a shift from trust in institutions to trust in hard assets.

## Understanding the Dow/Gold Ratio

### What It Measures

**Calculation:**
```
Dow/Gold Ratio = Dow Jones Industrial Average Closing Value ÷ Gold Price ($/oz)
```

**Interpretation:**
- **High Ratio (>20)**: Stocks expensive relative to gold; suggests equity overvaluation, risk-on sentiment, institutional trust
- **Medium Ratio (5-20)**: Balanced market; no extreme valuation signals
- **Low Ratio (<5)**: Gold expensive relative to stocks; suggests equity undervaluation or monetary crisis

### Historical Patterns

The ratio has fallen through critical levels three times in history: an 87% decline after 1929, a 92% decline from 1972-1980, and a 59% decline from 2007-2011, leading to exceptional gold price rises of 75%, 19x, and 2.5x respectively.

### What Drives the Ratio

**Stocks Rise (Ratio Increases) When:**
- Economic growth strong
- Corporate earnings expanding
- Inflation low and stable
- Interest rates moderate
- Trust in institutions high
- Risk appetite elevated
- Fiat currency confidence strong

**Gold Rises (Ratio Decreases) When:**
- Economic uncertainty elevated
- Currency debasement fears
- Inflation high or volatile
- Geopolitical crises
- Trust in institutions declining
- Financial system stress
- Negative real interest rates
