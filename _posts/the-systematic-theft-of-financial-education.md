For fifteen years, students memorize mathematical formulas they'll never use. They study chemical reactions they'll never witness and historical dates that have no bearing on their daily lives. This wasn't an oversight. It was by design.

The absence of financial education in schools isn't a pedagogical failure—it's social engineering. While students spend countless hours learning to calculate the area of complex geometric figures, they never learn to calculate the real cost of a loan. They memorize useless biological classifications but remain ignorant about how to diversify investments. They study extinct civilizations while their money loses value through inflation.

The brutal truth is this: the educational system didn't fail to teach financial literacy. It successfully avoided teaching it because financially ignorant populations are predictable, controllable, and infinitely profitable.

Those who lack financial knowledge follow a devastating pattern: work, spend everything, accumulate debt, work more to pay off debts, and repeat this cycle until death. Meanwhile, those who understand what was deliberately hidden from them accumulate wealth while sleeping. The difference between prosperity and poverty isn't effort—it's information. Information that was systematically withheld inside the classroom.
